About IRO

One office, two communities, every kind of issue.

The Issue Reporting Office exists so that no problem — big or small — goes unheard because no one knew where to send it. Whether you're a registered student or an indigene of the host community, IRO routes your report to the unit built to fix it, and keeps a paper trail so nothing gets forgotten.

Every report gets a ticket number the moment it's filed. That number is your receipt, your proof, and your way of checking progress without chasing anyone down a hallway.

  • Built for two groups.

    Students and indigenes report through the same form, sorted by category so the right unit sees it first.

  • Every report gets a ticket.

    A ticket ID like IRO-2026-001 is generated the moment you submit — keep it to track progress.

  • Anonymous by choice.

    Reports can be filed without a name attached, for issues you'd rather not put your identity to.

  • Routed to real units.

    Student Affairs, Security, and Works each pick up the categories that belong to them.

How it works

From report to resolved, in four stages.

Every ticket moves through the same pipeline, in this order.

STAGE 01

Submit

Fill in the report form with what happened, where, and who it affects. Choose to stay anonymous if you'd rather.

STAGE 02

Assigned

Your ticket is routed to the unit responsible for that issue type — Security, Works, or Student Affairs.

STAGE 03

In Progress

The assigned unit picks it up and works the case. You can check status anytime using your ticket ID.

STAGE 04

Resolved

Once fixed, the ticket is closed out with a final update — visible the next time you track it.

Got something to report right now?

It takes under two minutes, and you'll walk away with a ticket number.
